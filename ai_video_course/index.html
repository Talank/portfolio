AI Video Course

How to build an LLM-powered content-creation app, not just use one. The running product is DenDen Studio: give it a voice sample, a picture, and a description, and it generates an animated talking video — swap voices, speak into the mic and the picture speaks with you live, and fix any generated motion either by describing the change in plain words or by click-dragging it on a canvas. Everything runs on open-source models on your own machine — local LLMs via Ollama and Hugging Face, Whisper for speech-to-text, XTTS/Piper for voice cloning and TTS, Wav2Lip/SadTalker/LivePortrait-style talking heads, AnimateDiff-style text-guided motion. No paid model APIs anywhere. Taught the same way as the other courses: real mechanics, a One Piece story that acts the idea out (plus Big Bang Theory and Friends scenes), interactive animated diagrams, a practice lab, a quiz, and the interview questions companies actually ask.

Every lesson teaches the essentials by default — flip Essentials → Full Depth in the header any time you want a topic's deeper mechanism, edge cases, and "why it's actually built that way." Nothing is gated behind it; it's purely how far a lesson goes.

All music and sound effects are generated live in your browser with the Web Audio API — original compositions, nothing to license. Character portraits are original flat-vector art, not traced from any show.

How to use this

  1. This is a builder's course. The lessons teach every piece — the models, the pipeline, the editing UX, the backend — and you assemble DenDen Studio yourself on your own machine as you go. The course site itself stays small; the models live in your own Ollama/Hugging Face cache, downloaded only when you choose to.
  2. In each lesson: read the concept, open the story + animation the first time through, click through the interactive walkthrough, expand any Technicality corner question you can't already answer out loud, then do the Code Lab — small pure-Python pieces run right in the browser; model-dependent labs come with an exact "run it locally" recipe instead.
  3. Before marking a lesson complete, try the interview questions at the bottom out loud.
  4. Keep the Cheat Sheet open in a second tab — every model, command, and pipeline recipe in one place.
  5. Finish with the timed Interview Drill — randomized questions from everything you studied, on a countdown.
